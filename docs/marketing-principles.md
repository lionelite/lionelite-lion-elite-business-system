# Lion Elite Marketing Principles

## Use Familiar Buzzwords to Reframe the Conversation

The main premise for attracting clients is to start with a buzzword, trend, product category, or concept the audience already knows or has heard of, then elaborate on it in a different context that changes how they understand the problem and moves them toward the product.

The goal is not to repeat the same surface-level message everyone else is using. Familiar language earns attention; a new perspective earns trust and creates demand.

### Framework

1. **Lead with familiarity** — Open with a recognizable term people already search for, discuss, or associate with a desired result.
2. **Introduce a different context** — Challenge the common interpretation, expose what is missing, or connect the term to a deeper mechanism, problem, or opportunity.
3. **Create useful understanding** — Explain the concept clearly enough that the audience feels they learned something valuable.
4. **Bridge to the product** — Position the product as the logical next step, solution, tool, or application of the new perspective.
5. **Move the needle** — Every piece of content should change the audience’s belief, increase perceived value, and push them toward a specific action.

**Core sequence:** Familiar Buzzword → Unexpected Context → Education → Product Relevance → Call to Action

### Content Rule

Do not use a buzzword only for attention. The content must expand its meaning, connect it to the audience’s goals, and make the product more relevant.

### Examples of Familiar Entry Points

GLP-1s, metabolism, inflammation, recovery, longevity, peptides, collagen, biohacking, hormone balance, cellular health, fat loss, muscle growth, and anti-aging.

People stop because they recognize the topic. They stay because the perspective is different. They buy because the product now makes more sense.
