## First Batch Notes — Actual Production
- First batch produced 8 x 30 mL bottles, approximately 240 mL total.
- IMPORTANT CORRECTION: The entire 240 mL first batch used only 100 mg (0.10 g) total GHK-Cu, NOT 100 mg per 30 mL bottle.
- First-batch GHK-Cu concentration: approximately 0.0417% (100 mg / 240 mL).
- Approximate GHK-Cu per 30 mL bottle: 12.5 mg.
- Two separate L-arginine solution preparations were used during pH adjustment.
- During troubleshooting, approximately 20 mL was pulled from the main batch and combined with approximately 2 mL of arginine solution.
- That adjusted portion reached approximately pH 7.89 before being incorporated back into the main batch.
- Main-batch pH progression recorded during adjustment: approximately 4.37, then 4.52, then 5.25-5.26.
- After resting overnight in a dark area at room temperature, final recorded pH was approximately 5.02.
- Final appearance was clear blue with no visible separation.
- Final sensory notes: very liquid consistency, soft/slippery skin feel, hydrating feel, nearly but not fully sticky, odor could be improved.
- Exact grams of citric acid and L-arginine consumed in the two pH-adjustment solution preparations were not fully recorded and should not be guessed when calculating remaining inventory.

## GHK-Cu Concentration Decision
The first successful 240 mL batch used 100 mg total GHK-Cu. This equals approximately 0.417 mg/mL or 0.0417% GHK-Cu. The plan is to keep the scaled production run near this same concentration rather than arbitrarily increasing it until additional formulation/stability testing supports a change.

## Equipment Upgrade Plan for 100-Bottle Production
Instead of limiting production to the current 200 g scale and approximately 250 mL beaker, purchase larger production equipment before scaling.

Recommended equipment:
- Main mixing vessel: approximately 5 L borosilicate glass beaker or suitable 5 L 316 stainless-steel vessel
- Secondary mixing vessel: approximately 2 L borosilicate beaker
- Main production scale: approximately 5 kg capacity with 0.1 g resolution
- Fine precision scale: approximately 500 g capacity with 0.001 g resolution for GHK-Cu and low-weight ingredients
- Variable-speed overhead stirrer with an appropriate shaft/impeller
- Existing Apera pH meter can continue to be used
- Digital probe thermometer
- Graduated cylinders: approximately 100 mL, 500 mL, and 1,000 mL sizes
- Appropriate pipettes, syringes, and graduated transfer tools
- Manual or semi-automatic liquid bottle filler compatible with final serum viscosity
- 70% IPA and appropriate sanitation supplies
- Gloves and dedicated sanitized production workspace
- Light-protected bulk holding container if serum must be held before filling

A roughly 5 L main vessel is preferred for a 3.15-3.3 L production run because adequate headspace is needed for mixing without overflowing.

## Formulation / Compatibility Note
Before producing 100 retail units, verify the technical documentation for the exact GHK-Cu raw material and compatibility with the full formula. The first batch finished at approximately pH 5.02. Future production should maintain a validated target pH and confirm compatibility of GHK-Cu with the formula components, including Aloe Vera 200X and any acids/bases used for pH adjustment.
